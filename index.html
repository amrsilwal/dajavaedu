About Us

We Turn Study Abroad Dreams Into Reality

Da Java Education Consultancy was founded with a simple belief: every Nepali student deserves access to world-class education — and they deserve expert guidance to get there. We are Kathmandu's most trusted education consultancy, helping students navigate the path to studying abroad with clarity, confidence, and care.

From your first consultation to the day you land at your destination, we walk with you — choosing the right university, building a compelling application, securing your visa, and preparing you for life abroad.

Canada

World-renowned institutions, affordable tuition, and clear immigration pathways. The Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) allows up to 3 years of work, and Express Entry provides a clear road to permanent residency.

PGWP Up to 3 yrsExpress Entry PRWork While StudyingBilingual Culture

United Kingdom

Study at Oxford, Cambridge, UCL, and Edinburgh. The UK's Graduate Route Visa allows 2 years of post-study work, while shorter 1-year Masters degrees mean faster, cost-effective education with global recognition.

Malta

A hidden gem in Europe — Malta offers English-taught degrees at highly affordable costs, EU-recognised qualifications, and a safe Mediterranean lifestyle. Perfect for students seeking quality European education without premium costs.

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before starting your study abroad journey with Da Java.

  • Most universities require an English language test like IELTS, PTE, or TOEFL. Requirements vary — typically IELTS 6.0–6.5 for undergraduate and 6.5–7.0 for postgraduate. Our counselors will confirm exact requirements for your chosen course and help you plan your preparation.
  • Costs vary by country: Australia (AUD 20,000–45,000/yr), Canada (CAD 15,000–35,000/yr), UK (GBP 12,000–30,000/yr), and Malta (EUR 6,000–15,000/yr). We work with all budget levels and can identify scholarships to reduce your costs significantly.
  • Australia typically takes 4–8 weeks, Canada 8–16 weeks, UK 3–4 weeks, and Malta 4–8 weeks. We recommend starting at least 3–4 months before your intended intake. Our team manages timelines carefully to ensure nothing is delayed.
  • Yes! Australia allows up to 48 hours per fortnight, Canada up to 20 hours/week during term (full-time during breaks), UK up to 20 hours/week, and Malta allows part-time work for eligible students. Your counselor will explain specific work rights during your consultation.
  • Our visa team works hard to prevent rejections through meticulous preparation. In the rare event of a refusal, we analyse the reason, address the issues, and guide you through re-application or appeal. Our 95% first-attempt success rate reflects how rarely this occurs.
  • Yes — completely free with absolutely no obligation. We encourage all students to come in, discuss their situation, and understand all options before making any commitments. Call, WhatsApp, or book online to schedule your free session today.
  • The sooner the better! We recommend starting 6–12 months before your desired intake. This allows time for test preparation, university research, application, and visa processing. We also assist students with tighter timelines — just contact us.
