Australia

Home to 8 universities in the global top 100, Australia offers exceptional education, a multicultural society, and generous post-study work rights of up to 4 years. With strong demand for skilled migrants, it’s a top choice for students eyeing permanent residency.

Top 100 Universities2–4 yr Work VisaPR PathwaySafe & Multicultural
🇨🇦

Canada

Canada’s world-renowned institutions, affordable tuition, and clear immigration pathways make it a dream destination. The Post-Graduation Work Permit (PGWP) allows up to 3 years of work experience, and Express Entry provides a clear road to permanent residency.

PGWP Up to 3 yrsExpress Entry PRWork While StudyingBilingual Culture
🇬🇧

United Kingdom

Study at institutions like Oxford, Cambridge, UCL, and University of Edinburgh. The UK’s Graduate Route Visa allows 2 years of post-study work, while shorter degree durations — 1-year Masters — mean faster, cost-effective education with global recognition.

1-Year MastersGraduate Route VisaTop 10 World UnisGlobal Network
🇲🇹

Malta

A hidden gem in Europe — Malta offers English-taught degrees at highly affordable costs, EU-recognised qualifications, and a safe, sunny Mediterranean lifestyle. Increasingly popular for students seeking a quality European education without the premium price tag.

EU RecognisedLow Tuition FeesEnglish MediumSchengen Area

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before starting your study abroad journey with Da Java.

  • Most universities require an English language test like IELTS, PTE, or TOEFL. Minimum requirements vary by institution — typically IELTS 6.0–6.5 for undergraduate and 6.5–7.0 for postgraduate. Our counselors will confirm the exact requirements for your chosen course and help you plan your preparation.
  • Costs vary widely by country and course. As a rough guide: Australia (AUD 20,000–45,000/yr), Canada (CAD 15,000–35,000/yr), UK (GBP 12,000–30,000/yr), and Malta (EUR 6,000–15,000/yr). We work with students at various budget levels and can identify scholarship options to reduce your overall costs significantly.
  • Processing times vary by destination. Australia typically takes 4–8 weeks, Canada 8–16 weeks, UK 3–4 weeks, and Malta 4–8 weeks. We recommend starting your application at least 3–4 months before your intended intake. Our team manages timelines carefully to ensure nothing is ever delayed.
  • Yes! Australia allows up to 48 hours per fortnight, Canada up to 20 hours per week during term (full-time during breaks), UK up to 20 hours per week, and Malta allows part-time work for eligible students. Your counselor will explain the specific work rights for your destination during your consultation.
  • Our visa team works hard to prevent rejections through meticulous preparation. In the rare event of a refusal, we analyse the rejection reason, address the issues, and guide you through re-application or appeal. Our 95% first-attempt success rate reflects how rarely this occurs under our guidance.

However, we also assist students with tighter timelines — just contact us and we’ll find the best path forward for you.
